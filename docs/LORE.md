# Lore — Setting Bible

Skeleton. Fully populated by Phase 7 content tasks (CV-157, CV-158, CV-159).

## Setting

New York City, fall 1979. The blackout was last summer. The fiscal crisis bit hard. The trash hasn't been picked up in three weeks. Something is wrong with the rats.

## The premise (held loosely)

The Pest Control Division is a deniable city office. They give you a shotgun, a quota, and a list of complaints. They don't ask what's making the noises in the storm drains. They don't want to know why the pigeons on the bridge are getting larger. They want the noise to stop.

The player IS NOT a hero. The player IS the man with the shotgun who doesn't ask questions.

## Story is emergent

There is no scripted plot. Story emerges through collectible artifacts the player finds in the world:

- Tabloid clippings ("PIGEON-MAN OF BROOKLYN STRIKES AGAIN — Page 3")
- Sanitation Department internal memos ("Re: anomalous biomass observed at outflow site 27")
- Crayon drawings labeled "the rat man" by a kid in apartment 4B
- Payphone transcripts ("hi mom, i think there's something in the laundry")
- Matchbook covers from bars that maybe don't exist anymore
- Bodega owners' depositions
- Graffiti on subway tiles
- Flyers stapled to lampposts ("MISSING — Mittens, last seen by the F-train")

Each is a fragment. The player assembles the picture in their head. There is no Big Reveal. There is only an accumulating weight.

## Tone notes

Pulpy, not grim. The rat-king is awful but also kind of funny. The Pigeon-King at the end is genuinely menacing — but the cutscene that introduces him is a barfly's drunken pontification, not a doom-drone narration.

## What's known about the bestiary

See [docs/BESTIARY.md](BESTIARY.md). Each archetype has a "what people say about them" entry that goes in the Bestiary screen.

## What's intentionally unanswered

- Why is this happening?
- Who runs Pest Control?
- What's actually in the East River?
- What was the kid's note about?
- The Pigeon-King — metaphor or real?

The game ends when the player completes mission 12. There is no epilogue. Roll credits over the Brooklyn Bridge with the silhouette of something large taking off.
